<h2>STGraph - Samples & Examples</h2>


<b>Some models, suggesting a few ideas of what can be done with STGraph...</b>


<h3>Models of physical systems</h3>

- A simple model of a <a href="samples/physical_systems/ps1_bathtube_volumes.stg">bathtube</a> which level varies in time with inflow and outflow variations. 

- The <a href="samples/physical_systems/ps2_lorenz.stg">Lorentz's attractor</a>, also implemented as a<a href="samples/physical_systems/ps3_lorentz_int.stg">vector synchronous model</a>.

- A simple example showing <a href="samples/ps4_physical_systems/fft.stg">the power spectrum</a> of a noisy signal computed by the Fast Fourier Transform.

- In a completely polymorphic way, a model simulating <a href="samples/physical_systems/ps5_freebodies.stg">n bodies moving in a bounded 2D space</a>.

- A model showing the <a href="samples/physical_systems/ps6_brownian.stg">irreversibility of brownian motion</a> of n particles, in particular by displaying how the area of their bounding box statistically increases during the simulation.

- The <a href="samples/physical_systems/ps7_heat_equation.stg">ourier heat equation</a>, a simple partial differential equation.

- The example of an <a href="samples/physical_systems/ps8_fern_by_ifs.stg">iterated fractal system</a>.

- The example of an <a href="samples/physical_systems/ps9_damper.stg">damper</a> .

- A model showing the <a href="samples/physical_systems/ps10_gravitation_2d.stg">dynamic of a point</a> in a bidimensional gravity field.

- The example of a <a href="samples/physical_systems/ps11_mandelbrot.stg">Mandelbrot set</a>. 

- A model of <a href="samples/physical_systems/ps12_wave_equation_2D.stg">wave diffusion</a> in a two-dimension space.

<h3>Models of social systems</h3>

- The model of a  <a href="samples/social_systems/ss1_hom_firm.stg">firm</a> adjusting product price and quality to maximize profit.

- A model of <a href="samples/social_systems/ss2_innov_diff.stg">innovation diffusion</a>  where two firms compete for the same market with an undifferentiated product. 

- A simple model of <a href="samples/social_systems/ss3_project.stg">project management</a>.

- The model of an <a href="samples/social_systems/ss4_emergency_care.stg">emergency care room</a> to manage queue and personel cost.

- Some epidemiologic models, an <a href="samples/social_systems/ss5_epidemiologic_SIS.stg">hybrid SIR/SIS</a> and an <a href="samples/social_systems/ss6_epidemiologic_policy.stg">hybrid SIR/SIS with policy making</a>.

- An example of <a href="samples/social_systems/ss7_bottlenecks.stg">production process with bottlenecks</a>.

- A simple model of <a href="samples/social_systems/ss8_demand_forecast.stg">demand forecast</a>.

- A stock-flow calculator of <a href="samples/social_systems/ss9_inflation.stg">inflation effects on savings</a> during a given time range. 

- A model of <a href="samples/social_systems/ss10_price_demand_supply.stg">price, demand and supply dynamics</a> in a simple economy.

- An implementation of the classic<a href="samples/social_systems/ss11_el_farol.stg">El Farol bar problem</a>. 

- An example of how <a href="samples/social_systems/ss12_wealth_distribution.stg">inequalities</a> emerges from simple rules.

- A model simulating the <a href="samples/social_systems/ss13_spatial_market_2D.stg">behaviour of firms competing on a 2D spatially-defined market</a> without product differentiation.

- An implementation of the classic <a href="samples/social_systems/ss14_schelling.stg">Schelling segregation model</a>.

- A model that estimates <a href="samples/social_systems/ss15_football1.stg">football matches results</a> using expected goals, a <a href="samples/social_systems/ss16_football2.stg">more sophisticated second mode l</a> and a <a href="samples/social_systems/ss17_football3.stg">third model</a> that estimates the final standing of a championship simulating the results of missing matches.


<h3>Models of biological systems</h3>

- The prey-predator model depicted by Lotka-Volterra equations: a<a href="samples/biological_systems/bs1_lotka_volterra.stg">standard implementation</a>, a <a href="samples/biological_systems/bs2_lotka_volterra_inputs.stg">second implemtation</a>,where the user can modify the values of the stock, and a <a href="samples/biological_systems/bs3_lotka_volterra_hybrid.stg.stg">third implementation</a> where the population is distributed on a squared surface divided into cells and varies in time also according to stocks in nearer areas.

-The <a href="samples/biological_systems/bs4_verhulst.stg">Verhulst system</a>, , a classical dynamic system, which becomes chaotic when both the time step and the growth parameter are large enough. By modifying the growth parameter, the obtained behavior is either stable, oscillating, or fractal.

- A model of a <a href="samples/biological_systems/bs5_park.stg">city park ecosystem</a>, where four kind of entities affects each other in a non-trivial way.

- A model of  <a href="samples/biological_systems/bs6_expansion_1.stg">population expansion</a> following a logistic map, and an<a href="samples/biological_systems/bs7_expansion_2.stg">extension</a> where the maximum population size in each cell depends on the grass level (so, if the grass finishes, the population can estinguish).
